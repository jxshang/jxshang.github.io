Introduction

In recent years, with the flourish of online social media such as Facebook, Twitter, Weibo, TikTok, etc, the study of social networks has attracted a lot of researchers and finds its applications in many scenarios, such as social advertising, location-based social recommendation, popularity prediction, influential individual detection, et al. Benefit from the intelligent mobile devices, people are connecting more closely with each other than every before and are generating data at an unprecedented speed. These data include social connections, texts, images, audios, videos, rating data, geographic information, etc, both structured and unstructured. The variety and huge volume of available social big data has brought new challenges to the study of social network analysis and mining algorithms and applications.

DSONAM, standing in a data-drive point of view, aims to discuss and investigete the theories and methodologies of algorithms and applications realated to social networks from different disciplines such as computer science, data mining, machine learning, network science, sociology, and statistics in order to provide new insights on data-driven soial networks analysis and mining.

We sincerely invite researchers and practitioners to share their ideas, innovations, research achievements and solutions related to algorithms and applications of social networks analysis and mining. This workshop is co-located with IEEE (ISPA-2020, BDCloud-2020, SocialCom-2020, SustainCom-2020) International Conferences to be held in Exeter, England, UK, 15-17 October 2020.

Topics of interest include, but are not limited to:

  • Network representation learning on social networks;
  • Graph neural network and deep learning from social network data;
  • Knowledge graph related algorithms and applications;
  • Location-based social networks analysis and mining;
  • Machine learning and data mining from multimedia in social networks;
  • Network-based models and optimization algorithms;
  • Social influence analysis and information diffusion;
  • Recommendations and information retrieval in social networks;
  • Scalable models and algorithms for handling large-scale social network data;
  • Data-driven social network visualization;
  • Privacy, security, and robustness of social networks;
  • Data-driven network models and applications such as intelligent transport systems, financial risk analysis, mobile malware detection, etc.
